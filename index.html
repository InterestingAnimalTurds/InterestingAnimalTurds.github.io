Dialogue II

Based on my previous practice, I slowly realize that there is much to explore on the topic of Chinese people who are migrants, as immigrants, or as emigrants. Their accessibility to the local environment and vice versa. So I continued with a group of people, the immigrants from China, mostly from the Pearl-River-Delta area, and doing interviews within their context of the world. In this work, I hope to bring an unmediated, almost anthropological approach to the subject. Based on the pre-research, I construct a general question list. But as more interviews I did, it required me to reformed my prejudgement and assumption questions to adapt their context. These interviews would be displayed as a sound sculpture that creates an immersive soundscape. Audiences are invited to walk through a space with unfamiliarity. This space, at one point or another, simulates the feeling that the interviewee was or still is in American society. The dialogues are conducted in bilingual, even trilingual exchanges. And through the process, the idea of space in the dialogue is constantly shifting, re-orienting, and morphing in-between the context. But is the access to language really limited only by the physical geographical location? I am coming to realize that there is more to the matter than that, language usage is divided by location, by community, by culture, but beyond these, in context. However, it would request a longer pursue for this interview project as there are more ambiguous factors than I cannot currently make sense of. All of these factors push people to make a conscious choice of using spoken language. And the sound, as the media to sculpt the space. Language and culture become a contributor to the structure of the place. This work will be an ongoing project that does not end with a single outcome. I will keep working on it till there is an answer that is solid for me.
